Researching & Understanding The Story Of Enron:


My first step in my research was to build a solid understanding of the company, the scandal, and identify who the key players were.

General Information - What Was Enron?


Enron was an American energy company, based in Houston Texas, that was founded in 1985 by Kenneth Lay. The company was in the business of trading energy and comodities, and was considered one if the most innovative companies in the world at the time, and was even ranked as the 7th largest company in the by Fortune Magazine in early 2001. Enron had a variety of business partners, including but not limited to; UBS Warburg, Arthur Andersen LLP, McKinsey & Company, J.P. Morgan, Chase, Citigroup, Dynegy, Blockbuster, LJM Cayman, and the U.S. Coalition of Service Industries.

General Information - The Scandal Explained.


The scandal as a whole was the result of a variety of unethical and illegal practices that were being conducted by the company's executives that led to the company eventually declaring bankruptcy in late 2001 and thousands of employees losing their jobs and life savings. The main practices that were being conducted by the executive team included; mark-to-market accounting, which is the practice of recording potential future profits on the day a deal is signed, and off-balance-sheet entities, which are used to hide debt and inflate profits, and the use of special purpose entities, which are used to hide debt and inflate profits. These practices were used to create the illusion of a successful and profitable company, when in reality, the company was in financial trouble. The scandal was eventually uncovered by a whistleblower named Sherron Watkins, who was a Vice President at the company, and she alerted Kenneth Lay about the issues that were going on within the company. However, Lay did not take her seriously and did not take any action to address the issues.

Key Players Involved In The Scandal:


  • Kenneth Lay : Founder, Former CEO, and Chairman of Enron.
  • Jeffery Skilling : CEO of Enron at the time of the scandal.
  • Andrew Fastow : CFO of Enron.
  • Sheeron Watkins : Former Vice President of Enron, and Whistleblower.

Person Of Interest - Kenneth Lay:


Kenneth Lay was born April 15th, 1942 and died on July 5th, 2006 from a heart attack. He was the founder of Enron, and served as the Cheif Executive Officer (CEO) from 1985 until February of 2001. He was also a close friend of former U.S. President George W. Bush, and contributed heavily to his campaign. Lay received his education from the University of Houston, the University of Missouri, and the Robert J. Trulaske Sr. College of Business. In regards to the scandal, Lay was the mastermind behind the loopholes and accounting tricks that were used to; hide massive amounts of debt, inflate profits, and mislead investors, employees, and regulators about the company's financial condition. After his indictment, Kenneth Lay was convicted of one (1) count of Conspiracy, three (3) counts of Securities Fraud, two (2) counts of Wire Fraud, and faced a maximum sentence of twenty-five (25) to fourty-five (45) years in prison, but he died before he could serve any time in prison. He was posthumously pardoned by President George W. Bush in 2006, which was a controversial decision that was widely criticized by the public and legal experts alike.
